# Product Opportunities

This note stores product expansion ideas worth revisiting after the core launch
flows are stable. It is intentionally lightweight: each idea should earn its way
into a scoped spec before implementation.

## Suggested Priority

1. **Question Library** — Highest near-term leverage because it creates repeat
   value even when users do not have a new interview loop ready.
2. **Past-answer Journal** — Strong retention feature that helps users see
   progress over repeated practice.
3. **Company Prep Dossier** — High perceived value, but depends on reliable web
   research and source quality.
4. **Mock Interview Replay** — Differentiated, but larger technical and privacy
   surface because it introduces audio, transcription, and storage decisions.
5. **Shareable Scorecard** — Useful for referrals and coaching, best added once
   scoring outputs feel consistently trustworthy.

## Ideas

### Question Library

Build a browsable, role-tagged bank of interview questions that users can filter
by company type, seniority, function, and interview stage.

- **Why it matters:** Gives users something useful to do between full prep
  sessions and makes the product feel content-rich.
- **User value:** Quick drills, warmups, and targeted practice without starting a
  new generated session.
- **Risk / effort:** Medium. Requires taxonomy, quality control, and avoiding
  generic filler questions.

### Past-answer Journal

Save selected answers across sessions so users can revisit, re-answer, and
compare improvement over time.

- **Why it matters:** Turns progress into a visible loop instead of a static
  score page.
- **User value:** Helps users see better structure, sharper examples, and reduced
  repetition over time.
- **Risk / effort:** Medium. Needs clear privacy expectations and careful UX so
  it does not become clutter.

### Company Prep Dossier

Generate a concise company prep brief from a company URL or name, including
recent news, likely interview themes, public product context, and possible hiring
signals.

- **Why it matters:** Users often need company-specific prep, not only role
  practice.
- **User value:** Saves research time and makes generated questions feel more
  tailored.
- **Risk / effort:** High. Depends on source quality, freshness, citations, and
  cost controls for web research.

### Mock Interview Replay

Let users record spoken answers, then transcribe and review delivery signals such
as pacing, filler words, clarity, and answer structure.

- **Why it matters:** Differentiates InterviewIntel from text-only prep tools.
- **User value:** Simulates real interviews more closely and improves speaking
  confidence.
- **Risk / effort:** High. Requires audio UX, transcript handling, storage,
  privacy policy review, and likely new billing/usage limits.

### Shareable Scorecard

Create a polished, optional scorecard users can export or share after a session.
It could summarize strengths, improvement areas, and recommended next drills.

- **Why it matters:** Provides a natural referral loop and a coaching artifact.
- **User value:** Gives users a clear takeaway they can save, compare, or share
  with mentors.
- **Risk / effort:** Low to medium. The biggest risk is sharing scores before the
  scoring rubric is stable enough to feel fair.
